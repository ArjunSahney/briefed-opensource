### Supreme Court Decision on Texas Immigration Law

The Supreme Court has permitted Texas to implement SB 4, a law allowing police to arrest individuals suspected of crossing the Mexico-Texas border illegally, despite ongoing legal battles regarding its constitutionality. This divisive decision, supported by the court's conservative majority but opposed by its liberal justices, highlights a significant moment in the contentious debate over state versus federal jurisdiction in immigration matters. While the federal government, alongside several advocacy groups, argues that the law infringes upon exclusively federal immigration powers, Texas officials contend it is a necessary measure for state defense against criminal activities linked to illegal crossings. The law's enforcement could reshape immigration enforcement dynamics and impact U.S.-Mexico relations, amidst a complex legal landscape set for further exploration in the courts (Article 1, Article 2, Article 3).

### Sentencing of Mississippi 'Goon Squad'

In a landmark case, two former Mississippi law enforcement officers, Hunter Elward and Jeffrey Middleton, were sentenced to around 20 and 17.5 years respectively, for racially motivated torture of two Black men, Michael Corey Jenkins and Eddie Terrell Parker. The victims suffered a brutal attack initiated after a racially charged complaint. The case highlighted egregious violation of duty by the 'Goon Squad', leading to significant sentences justified by the severity of their actions (Article 2, Article 3).
