### Diplomatic Efforts to Resolve Ukraine Conflict

Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy's visit to Turkey for talks with President Recep Tayyip Erdogan highlights Turkey's crucial mediator role between Ukraine and Russia. During this significant meeting, Erdogan is expected to advocate for negotiations to end the ongoing conflict between Ukraine and Russia, now in its third year (Article 1). The discussions are also set to address Black Sea navigation safety, a concern for global food stability given the region's role in shipping Ukrainian grain. Despite Turkey's close relations with both Moscow and Kyiv, Erdogan's efforts underscore a delicate balance, reflecting Turkey's broader strategic intentions to support Ukraine's sovereignty while maintaining a pivotal position in the conflict dynamics (Article 2, Article 3).

### Turkey's Role in the Ukraine Crisis

During his visit to Istanbul, Ukrainian President Zelenskiy engaged with Turkish President Erdogan, indicating Turkey's nuanced position amid the ongoing Ukraine-Russia conflict (Article 1, Article 3). Erdogan's administration strives to mediate peace while supporting Ukraine's territorial integrity, showcasing a delicate balance between aiding Kyiv and maintaining relations with Moscow (Article 3). Despite Russia's withdrawal from the Black Sea grain deal and ongoing hostilities, Turkey continues its diplomatic efforts for negotiations and safe navigation in the Black Sea, emphasizing its strategic yet humanitarian approach to resolving the crisis (Article 1, Article 3).
