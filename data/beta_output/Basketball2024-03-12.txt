**March to Madness: DII & DI Championships Kick Off**

The 2024 NCAA Division II Men's Basketball Championship is set to begin with a 64-team field aiming for glory in Evansville, Indiana, from March 16-30. Notably, Nova Southeastern celebrated its first national championship victory in 2023 (Article 1). Concurrently, Texas Woman's University (TWU) gears up to host the NCAA DII regional quarterfinal tournament, marking a historic occasion as they clinched the top spot at the Lonestar Conference Championships (Article 2). On the Division I front, CBS Sports and TNT Sports detailed announced teams for the widespread coverage of the Men's Basketball Championship, featuring new commentators and initiating play-by-play announcer Ian Eagle's debut at the Final Four (Article 3).

### Big Ten 2024 Basketball Honors Summary

The Big Ten Conference has unveiled its 2024 men's basketball postseason awards, highlighting Purdue's Zach Edey as the Player of the Year for the second consecutive time—a rare achievement shared with only three other players since 2000. Edey's impressive season averages of 24.2 points, 11.7 rebounds, and 2.2 blocks greatly contributed to Purdue securing the Big Ten regular season title again. Purdue coach Matt Painter and Nebraska's Fred Hoiberg were named Co-Coaches of the Year, with Painter achieving his fifth such accolade and Hoiberg significantly improving Nebraska’s performance. The all-conference selections showcased talents like Illinois’ Terrence Shannon Jr., Northwestern’s Boo Buie, and others for their remarkable seasons (Article 2).
