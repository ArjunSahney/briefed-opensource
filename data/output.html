Trump Seeks New Judge in Hush-Money Case Amid Legal Challenges
Former President Donald Trump is making a last-minute effort to have a new judge preside over his upcoming hush-money criminal trial, citing a conflict of interest due to the current judge's connections and accusing him of bias. Key Points on Northeast Earthquake and Aftershocks
The Northeast experienced a rare 4.8-magnitude earthquake, with the USGS recording 28 aftershocks, the largest being a 3.8-magnitude event near Gladstone, New Jersey. Officials advise adopting safety measures during quakes, emphasizing the 'drop, cover, and hold on' technique for protection. Over 23 million people felt the tremors, with reports of shaking buildings and assessments for structural stability underway (FOX Weather; CNN). No major injuries or infrastructure damage have been reported, and residents are encouraged to continue their normal routines (NBC News).

Trudeau Announces Major Funding for Homebuilding Initiatives Amid Political Criticism
Prime Minister Justin Trudeau announced a $600 million funding package to accelerate home construction and rental projects across Canada, aiming to address the pressing housing crisis (CBC). The announcement was met with skepticism by Alberta Premier Danielle Smith, who criticized the federal government for overstepping provincial jurisdiction and politicizing the distribution of funds based on political alliances rather than need (CTV News Calgary). Trudeau's push in the housing sector comes amidst opposition pressure, notably from Pierre Poilievre, positioning the government to play a more active role in housing than in the past (CTV News Calgary).

Alberta Fights Aquatic Invasive Species
Alberta has allocated $2.5 million to enhance watercraft inspection and detection to combat aquatic invasive species, including whirling disease, which threatens fish populations. This follows Parks Canada's action of closing Yoho and Kootenay national parks to certain water activities until 2025 (St. Albert Gazette).
